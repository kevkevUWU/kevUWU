What is a Croissant?

A croissant is a French pastry known for its flaky outer crust and soft interior. Its crescent shape resembles a crescent moon and is typically made with butter and flour.

Historical Background

The croissant originated in Austria but was introduced to France in the 19th century, quickly becoming a part of French café culture. Today, it is a popular breakfast choice worldwide.

How to Make

Follow these steps to make a delicious croissant:

  1. Prepare the dough and butter.
  2. Wrap the butter in the dough and roll it out multiple times.
  3. Cut into triangles and roll up.
  4. Let rise, then bake in the oven.
  5. Enjoy your freshly baked croissants!
